# Unidata Summer Intern Program

### METAR and Surface Map Project
I worked with Ryan May on adding a METAR parser to the MetPy library. The reason
for working on this project is because of the primary issues within the meteorology
Python community is the lack of quality surface observations that can be used
to create surface station plots. Speaking from personal experience and knowledge,
at Valparaiso University, as of spring of 2019, GEMPAK (a data visualization program that utilizes Fortran)
is still used for creating surface maps in the Weather Technology course. <br/>

The first step was creating a parser that could separate lines of text into
different variables. For this part of the project, I used Canopy, a parser generator
that utilizes a grammar file and is flexible enough to parse the essential information
from the METAR observation. <br/>

### Workshops
The second week of the internship program, I traveled to Albany, New York to serve
as an instructor for the Unidata Regional Workshop. The workshop was held in the map
at the University at Albany in the Atmospheric Science Map Room. Attendees ranged
from graduate students and professors to private sector meteorologists. <br/>

I will also be aiding with the Unidata Student Workshop at the American Meteorological
Society Student Conference held in Boston, Massachusetts. This workshop will last
about 2 hours, and up to 200 students will be able to attend. We will cover a wide
range of topics from data read in, to generating publication quality graphics.
